Private Journals

Excerpts from the desks of our members

Dawn in the Sahara

The dunes turned rose before the sun arrived. We walked in silence, footprints erased by wind before we looked back. In that hour, nothing existed but sand and breath.

— E. Whitmore, Member since 2022

Winter in Kyoto

Snow settled on temple roofs like powdered sugar. The garden keeper swept a single path through white silence. We understood then why the Japanese have a word for the beauty of impermanence.

— M. Laurent, Member since 2021

Midnight in Iceland

The aurora began as a whisper of green, then unfurled across the sky like silk caught in wind. We stood on black volcanic sand and felt the earth breathe beneath us.

— A. Chen, Member since 2023

Dear Mavoro,

I have traveled with many clubs, but none understood that the journey begins long before the plane lands. Mavoro gave me Patagonia not as a checklist, but as a meditation. I returned quieter, and richer for it.

— Victoria Ashford

Dear Mavoro,

The Kyoto winter retreat changed how I see time itself. No itinerary could have prepared me for sitting with a master calligrapher while snow fell on cedar. This is what travel should be.

— James Whitfield

Dear Mavoro,

What struck me most was the respect — for landscapes, for cultures, for the pace of discovery. The Zanzibar journey felt like being welcomed into a story already in progress. I am forever grateful.

— Amara Osei
